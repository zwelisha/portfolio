Theo Tours Website and Booking System

Theo Tours (www.theotours.co.za) wanted their website to be revamped for the company to be able to fully automate the business process. Firstly, I had to redesign the entire website and add additional pages such as the internships page for tourism students who would love to do internships at Theo Tours. The page allows the interns to apply online to be shortlisted for interviews. Secondly, the company wanted all tour bookings to be done online and clients to get instant feedback. Thirdly, I had to work on SEO (Search Engine Optimization) to ensure the company ranks higher on Google and appear first in google search results for related search queries. Finally, Theo, the company owner wanted his clients to write Trip Advisor reviews on the website after each and every tour. I accomplished his requirements by checking the API documentation of Trip Advisor for website plugins and added a section that displays Trip Advisor live reviews. In addition, I added a section where tourists could write reviews using their Trip Advisor accounts. This was another individual project that I enjoyed the most. As a big fan of PHP, I used PHP and MySQL for back-end, Bootstrap for front-end and xampp server for development.

2017 Standard Bank IT Challenge (South African Universities Programming Olympiad) - 3rd Place

This was a blockchain based competition where we were given 48 hours to identify an existing problem and implement a blockchain based solution to solve the identified problem. My team and I created a system that enables financial donors to audit all charity organisations they donate the money to by tracking all their transactions. This was an exciting project since I worked with people I met at the competition. Standard Bank decided to shuffle the teams, as a result there was not even a single member from Rhodes University in my team for the IdeaChain, which made me a bit uncomfortable. However, the most exciting part was leading the team which involved drawing system diagrams, clarifying concepts in isiXhosa to other team members and equally dividing the work. In addition, I was responsible for coding the whole front-end part of the system since there were only six members in the team, and only three of us had experience in practical software development other than basic school practicals. Even though, the whole project was stressful, it was exciting to see how fast paced the software development industry is in the real world. The support of companies such as Consensys and Luno made the whole process easier.

The 2016 International Collegiate Programming Contest - Top 30 (26th in Africa, 3rd at Rhodes University)

The International Collegiate Programming Contest is an algorithmic programming contest for college students. It is by far the most fierce and rigorously contested programming challenge for college students. Teams of three, representing their university, work to solve the most real-world problems, fostering collaboration, creativity, innovation, and the ability to perform under pressure. This hackathon, is not only about submitting solutions that work. Submitted algorithms are timed for efficiency. In 2016, my our team (Team Y Nots) came 3rd at Rhodes University and 26th in Africa. It was a huge success considering the strong competition brought by UCT, WITS, UP and Teams from other African Universities, since there wer more than 90 teams in total.
